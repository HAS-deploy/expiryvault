sell by date — what it means for shelf life

What it means

A sell by date is a label placed on food products by manufacturers to tell retailers how long to display an item for sale. It is primarily an inventory management and stock rotation tool — not a safety cutoff. Once this date passes, the store is expected to remove the product from shelves, but the food itself is not necessarily spoiled or unsafe to eat.

In most countries, sell by dates are not federally regulated for most food categories, meaning they reflect the manufacturer's best estimate of peak quality rather than any government-certified safety threshold.

Why it matters

This date comes up in two main situations: when you're shopping and notice a product is dated for today or tomorrow, and when you find something in your fridge or pantry that's just past its sell by date. Many people throw away perfectly good food because they misread this label as an expiration date. The USDA estimates that date label confusion is a significant driver of household food waste. Understanding what "sell by" actually means can save money and reduce waste without putting anyone at risk.

Retailers use it operationally — a product might have a sell by date of Monday but remain safe and good quality through Wednesday or Thursday, depending on the item. Dairy, deli meats, eggs, and packaged meats are common products where this label appears.

Example

A carton of milk is stamped Sell By: June 12. The grocery store pulls it from the shelf on June 12. A customer who bought it on June 10 can reasonably expect it to stay fresh in a properly refrigerated fridge until around June 14–15. The sell by date told the store when to stop selling it — not when the consumer must stop drinking it.

Common confusions

  • Sell by vs. use by: A use by date is directed at the consumer and carries more weight as a quality or safety indicator. You should pay closer attention to use by dates, especially on items like deli meats or baby formula.
  • Sell by vs. best by: A best by (or best before) date indicates peak quality for the consumer — flavor and texture may decline after that point, but the product is often still safe. Sell by is store-facing; best by is consumer-facing.
  • Treating it as an expiration date: A true expiration date means the product should not be consumed after that point. Sell by dates do not mean this. Conflating the two leads to unnecessary food waste.
